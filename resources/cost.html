What Affects SMP Cost?

1. Size of Treatment Area

  • Small (hairline only, small scar): Lower cost
  • Medium (partial density, crown + hairline): Mid-range
  • Large (full scalp, complete baldness): Full price

2. Complexity of Hair Loss Pattern

  • Irregular patterns require more time and precision
  • Blending with existing hair is more complex than full baldness
  • Scar tissue may require additional sessions

3. Practitioner Experience & Reputation

  • Highly experienced artists (500+ procedures) charge premium rates
  • You're paying for skill, artistry, and proven results
  • Warning: Suspiciously low prices often mean inexperience

4. Geographic Location

  • Toronto/Vancouver: $3,000 - $4,000+ (higher cost of living)
  • Calgary/Edmonton: $2,500 - $3,200 (mid-range)
  • Smaller Cities: $2,200 - $2,800 (lower overhead)

5. Number of Sessions Required

  • Most treatments: 3 sessions
  • Complex cases or darker desired look: 4 sessions
  • Touch-ups at 3-5 year intervals

FAQ: SMP Cost Questions

Is SMP covered by insurance in Canada?

No. SMP is considered a cosmetic procedure and is not covered by provincial health insurance or most private insurance plans. However, if SMP is used to address psychological distress from medical hair loss (e.g., alopecia), some flexible spending accounts may cover it‚Äîcheck with your insurer.

Reputable practitioners have set pricing based on their expertise and overhead. However, some offer referral discounts, package deals, or financing options. Avoid practitioners who significantly discount their services‚Äîit may indicate lack of experience.

While prices may be lower in smaller cities, factor in travel, accommodation, and the inconvenience of multiple trips (sessions are spaced 10-14 days apart). Choose based on practitioner quality, not just price.
