Does SMP Hurt? What It Feels Like and Numbing Options

Let's address the elephant in the room: Yes, SMP involves needles penetrating your skin. But here's the honest truth about what it actually feels like.

Quick Answer

Pain Level: 3-5 out of 10 (varies by individual)

Feels Like: Mild scratching, light sunburn being touched, or eyebrow microblading

Numbing Available: Yes‚Äîtopical numbing cream significantly reduces discomfort

Aftercare Pain: Minimal‚Äîslight tenderness for 1-2 days

Pain Varies by Scalp Area

Not all areas of your scalp have the same sensitivity:

More Sensitive Areas (Higher Discomfort)

  • Temples: Thinner skin, closer to bone
  • Crown (top of head): More nerve endings
  • Near Ears: Sensitive, delicate skin
  • Sides of Head: Thinner tissue

Less Sensitive Areas (Lower Discomfort)

  • Back of Head: Thicker skin, fewer nerve endings
  • Mid-Scalp: Generally less reactive

Pain Management Options

1. Topical Numbing Cream

The most common and effective pain management method:

  • Applied 30-45 minutes before the procedure
  • Can be reapplied during the session
  • Significantly reduces sensation (most clients report 50-70% reduction)
  • Safe, non-invasive, and widely used

2. Breaks During the Session

  • Sessions typically last 3-4 hours
  • You can request breaks at any time
  • Stand up, walk around, get water, reset mentally
  • No rush‚Äîyour comfort matters

3. Comfort Protocols

Experienced practitioners adjust technique based on your feedback:

  • Adjust needle depth (shallower = less discomfort)
  • Modify speed and pressure
  • Work on less sensitive areas first to build tolerance
  • Communicate openly if you need adjustments

4. Breathing & Relaxation Techniques

  • Deep breathing reduces tension and perceived pain
  • Distraction (music, conversation) helps significantly
  • Mindfulness techniques (focus on breathing, not sensation)

Pain During vs. After SMP

During the Procedure

  • First 30-60 minutes: Most uncomfortable (fresh, sensitive skin)
  • Mid-session: Numbing kicks in; discomfort decreases
  • Final hour: Skin may feel tender, but tolerable

Immediately After (First Few Hours)

  • Redness: Normal and expected
  • Tenderness: Similar to a mild sunburn
  • Slight Swelling: Rare, but possible in sensitive individuals

Days 1-4

  • Minimal discomfort‚Äîmost clients report none
  • Scalp may feel tight as it heals
  • No pain medication typically needed

Day 5 and Beyond

  • Discomfort fully resolved
  • Healing continues, but painlessly

Who Tolerates SMP Pain Best?

Higher Pain Tolerance:

  • People who've had tattoos before
  • Those with previous cosmetic procedures (microblading, etc.)
  • Individuals who practice mindfulness or meditation

May Experience More Discomfort:

  • First-time procedure clients (anxiety amplifies pain perception)
  • Highly sensitive individuals
  • Those with low pain tolerance

Good News: Even clients with low pain tolerance successfully complete SMP with numbing and breaks.

FAQ: SMP Pain Questions

Is SMP more painful than a tattoo?

No. SMP needles penetrate less deeply than traditional tattoo needles (dermal layer vs. deeper tissue). Most clients report SMP as less painful than body tattoos.

Can I take pain medication before SMP?

Avoid blood thinners (aspirin, ibuprofen, alcohol) for 48 hours before your session‚Äîthey increase bleeding and affect pigment retention. Acetaminophen (Tylenol) is generally safe. Always consult your practitioner first.

Will I need pain medication afterwards?

Rarely. Most clients experience only mild tenderness that doesn't require medication. If needed, acetaminophen (Tylenol) is safe for post-procedure discomfort.

Does pain get worse with each session?

Usually, no. Many clients report the first session as the most uncomfortable due to anxiety and unfamiliarity. Subsequent sessions are often easier because you know what to expect.

Can I stop the procedure if it's too painful?

Absolutely. Reputable practitioners prioritize your comfort and will pause or reschedule if you're genuinely uncomfortable. However, this is extremely rare with proper numbing.
